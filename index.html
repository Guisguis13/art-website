Black and White Traditional Indigenous Artist | Ryley, AB

Welcome and Thank You for visiting Vance Heslin Artistry. Vance's Gallery primarily contains Indigenous Artwork commonly composed of Black and White artwork. Always having a nack for beautiful geometric designs, Vance decided it was the perfect way to bring his imagined scenes together. Please take the time to enjoy his collection and learn a little about this small town artist.

Vance was born in Montreal on July 9th 1969. Growing up, he spent most of his time in Ontario southern with his Mother and 6 sisters. He met his loving wife Cyndy in 1994 and married her the following year and at the same time added Justin to his family. Shortly after Vance had two more children named Tyler and Jared. Soon after Jared was born in 1999 his blooming family moved out west to the small village of Holden in 2003. In 2008 his family moved to Ryley where he lives now with only his wife of more than 25 years after all his kids have grown up and moved out.
Vance started drawing in 7th Grade as just doodles on his unfinished school work. But has progressed over time into beautifully drawn love letters to scathing renditions of unpopular teachers. Into this early adult years life took its toll on Vance's free time to draw and only came back to it just before he met his Wife. He loves to take his to time with his artwork, a true labour of love with many painstaking hours to create his artwork. Now with retirement just around the corner, he has not only the free time but the inspiration that only comes with maturity and wisdom.

Vance's art as described earlier is Primarily Black and White Traditional Indigenous artwork. His technique is very thorough and farsighted.

Laying out the drawing to scale in pencil and gets more and more detailed as the drawing progresses. After all the lines are drawn and the details filled, Vance uses a variety of markers of different densities and thickness to finish the artwork.
The question all artists are asked and is probably to hardest to answer for many is, why do you draw? Why do you love it so much? For Vance, it's an easy answer... "Because I like to!" In all seriousness, he loves creating art as a way of self-expression. To produce art he is passionate. To give each creation his heart. His happiness, his wonder, his sadness and his love.
