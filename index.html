Black and White Traditional Indigenous Artist | Ryley, AB

Welcome and Thank You for visiting Vance Heslin Artistry. Vance's Gallery primarily contains Indigenous Artwork commonly composed of Black and White Artwork. Always having a nack for beautiful, geometric designs; Vance decided it was the perfect way to bring his imagined scenes together. Please take the time to enjoy his collection, and learn a little about this small town artist.

Vance was born in Montreal on July 9th, 1969. Growing up, he spent most of his time in southern Ontario with his Mother and six sisters. He met his loving wife, Cyndy, in 1994, and married her the following year. At the same time he welcomed his first son Justin to the family. Shortly after Vance had two more children named Tyler (1996) and Jared (1999). His blooming family moved out west to the small village of Holden in 2003. In 2008, his family moved to Ryley, where he lives now, with his wife of more than 25 years.
Vance started drawing in 7th Grade as just doodles on his unfinished school work. This progressed into beautifully drawn love letters and scathing renditions of unpopular teachers. Early into his adult years, life took its toll on Vance's free time to draw. Only coming back to it just before he met his Wife. He loves to take his to time with his artwork, a true labour of love with many painstaking hours to create his original pieces. Now with retirement just around the corner, he has not only the free time but the inspiration that only comes with maturity and wisdom.
Vance's art, as described earlier, is primarily Black and White Traditional Indigenous Artwork. His technique is very thorough and farsighted. Laying out the drawing to scale in pencil, he gets more and more detailed as the drawing progresses. After all the lines are drawn and the details are filled, Vance uses a variety of markers of different densities and thicknesses to finish the artwork.
The question all artists are asked (and is probably one of the hardest to answer) is; "Why do you draw? Why do you love it so much?". For Vance, it's an easy answer... "Because I like to!". In all seriousness, he loves creating art as a way of self-expression. To produce art he is passionate about. To give each creation a piece of his heart, his happiness, his wonder, his sadness and his love.
